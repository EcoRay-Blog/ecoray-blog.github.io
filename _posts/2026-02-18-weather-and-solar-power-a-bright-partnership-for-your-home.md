## How Weather Impacts Solar Panels

It’s a common myth that *solceller* only work in sunny climates. In fact, solar panels generate electricity from daylight—not just direct sunshine. This means that even under cloud cover, your *solcelleanlæg* is still producing energy. Here’s how different weather affects your system:

- **Cloudy Days:** Solar panels capture diffused sunlight, so you’ll still see returns on those overcast Scandinavian afternoons.
- **Rain and Snow:** Rain helps clean panels, maintaining their efficiency. Snow may temporarily reduce output, but panels are installed at an angle to help snow slide off naturally.
- **Cold Weather:** Solar panels actually perform better in cool temperatures, so the crisp winters in Norway or Minnesota can boost efficiency.

## Solar in Northern Climates

You might wonder if *solceller til hus* make sense in Denmark or Sweden, where winters are long and daylight is short. The answer is a resounding yes! Longer summer days and modern *solcelleanlæg* designs mean you can store energy and use it during darker periods—especially if you choose *solceller med batteri*.
