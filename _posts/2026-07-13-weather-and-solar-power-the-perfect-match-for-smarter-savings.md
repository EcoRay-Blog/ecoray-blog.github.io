## How Weather Impacts Solar Panels

It’s a common myth that solar panels only work in blazing sunshine. In fact, today’s **best solcelleanlæg** are engineered to perform efficiently in a range of weather conditions—from crisp Scandinavian mornings to sunny afternoons in California. Here’s how different weather types affect your **solceller**:

- **Cloudy Days:** Modern **solceller til hus** (solar panels for houses) still generate electricity even when it’s overcast. While output may dip, your panels keep working—often more efficiently in cooler temperatures.
- **Snow:** In regions like Norway or Sweden, snow can reflect sunlight, giving panels a boost when they’re clear. Plus, snow often slides off smooth glass surfaces quickly.
- **Rain:** Showers can actually clean your panels, removing dust and helping maintain efficiency.
- **Long Summer Days:** In places like Denmark and Sweden, those extra daylight hours in summer mean your **solcelleanlæg** can capture more energy than you might expect.

Solceller i Danmark, Sverige og Norge er designet til at fungere effektivt under forskellige vejrforhold, inklusive overskyede dage og kolde vintre. Moderne solcelleanlæg udnytter også reflekteret lys fra sne, og lavere temperaturer kan øge panelernes ydeevne.
