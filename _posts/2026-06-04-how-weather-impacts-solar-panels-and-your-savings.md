## The Surprising Truth About Solar and Weather

Many believe that only sunny, warm climates are suitable for solar panels. In reality, modern solceller work efficiently even on cloudy days and in colder regions. Here’s what you need to know:

- **Cloudy Days:** Yes, solar panels still generate electricity when it’s cloudy—just less than on a clear day. On average, solceller can produce 10-25% of their typical output during overcast conditions.
- **Cold Weather:** Solar panels actually perform better in cooler temperatures. Extreme heat can reduce efficiency, so Denmark, Sweden, and Norway are perfect regions for high-performing solcelleanlæg.
- **Snow:** Light snow can reflect sunlight, increasing energy production. Heavy snow, however, can temporarily block sunlight until it slides or melts off. Most installations are designed for easy snow shedding.
- **Rain:** Rain helps by cleaning dust and pollen off your panels, keeping them efficient.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

### 1. Hvordan påvirker vejr effektiviteten af solceller?
Solceller producerer elektricitet selv på overskyede dage, men med lavere effektivitet (10-25% mindre). Kolde temperaturer forbedrer faktisk ydeevnen, mens sne kan både øge og midlertidigt reducere produktionen afhængigt af mængden.

### 2. Kan jeg bruge solceller med batteri til at lagre energi under dårligt vejr?
Ja, solceller med batteri gør det muligt at lagre overskydende energi, så du kan bruge din egen strøm, selv når solen ikke skinner, hvilket øger din selvforsyning og sparer på elregningen.
