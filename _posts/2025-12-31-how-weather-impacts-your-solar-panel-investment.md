## Sunlight, Clouds, and Your Solar Returns

It’s a common myth that solceller only work well in hot, sunny climates. In reality, modern solcelleanlæg are designed to perform efficiently even in the varying weather conditions found across Scandinavia and North America.

- **Cloudy days:** While peak production happens under direct sunlight, today’s intelligent solar systems can still generate significant electricity on overcast days. Your solceller may actually operate better in cooler temperatures, as excessive heat can slightly reduce efficiency.
- **Snow and rain:** Light snow typically slides off panels, especially if they’re installed at the correct angle. Rain helps keep your solceller clean, washing away dust and debris for optimal performance.
- **Winter performance:** In Nordic regions, shorter days mean less production in winter, but long summer days often compensate for this. Many homeowners and businesses find that their yearly energy savings remain impressive.
