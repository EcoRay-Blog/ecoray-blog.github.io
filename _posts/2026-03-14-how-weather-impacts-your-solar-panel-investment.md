## Sun, Clouds, and Everything In Between

Solar panels are designed to capture sunlight and convert it into electricity, which means the amount of sunshine matters. But that doesn’t mean you need endless summer to benefit from solceller. Here’s how various weather factors come into play:

- **Sunny Days:** Naturally, solceller til hus (home solar panels) produce the most energy on clear, sunny days. If you live in a region with long daylight hours, you’ll notice higher energy production and bigger savings.
- **Cloudy Weather:** Even on overcast days, modern solar panels can generate power. Diffused sunlight still reaches your panels, though energy output will be lower than on bright days.
- **Rain and Snow:** While rain might seem like a drawback, it actually helps by cleaning dust and debris off your panels, maintaining their efficiency. Snow can temporarily block sunlight, but panels are installed at angles that help snow slide off. Plus, cold weather can even improve solar panel efficiency!
- **Seasonal Variations:** In Scandinavia and northern parts of the USA, winter brings shorter days, but the long summer days provide ample sunlight, balancing out annual production.
